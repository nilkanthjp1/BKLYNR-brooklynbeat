    How Brooklyn is Covered

    Intuition tells us that, over the course of the past few decades, Brooklyn has received a great deal more attention than it used to. There exist numerous explanations for this rise—multiple development initiatives, a renewed emphasis on strengthening the borough’s middle class, and even the waves of violence in the wake of the racially-charged killings that plagued Brooklyn’s early nineties. We decided to take a look at those changes and quantify Brooklyn’s entry into the collective mindset of New York City residents by using the The New York Times Article Search API to analyzing how they have adjusted their coverage since 1981. Going beyond just the number of times “Brooklyn” has been mentioned by the paper of record, we also pulled data on individual neighborhoods, to see which areas were dominating coverage. All of this information has been assembled into the tidy visualization hiding behind this page. Click the arrow below to begin exploring.

    By Nilkanth Patel
